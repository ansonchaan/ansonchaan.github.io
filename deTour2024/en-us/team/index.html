Curator-at-large

Shin Wong

Shin Wong isn't just a curator—she's a creative powerhouse redefining Hong Kong's art and design landscape with her bold and fearless approach. With over 15 years of experience challenging the boundaries of entertainment, fashion, and the arts, Shin's name has become synonymous with innovation, conceptual brilliance, and impactful artist collaborations.

Since assuming leadership of the Hong Kong Design Festival ‘deTour' in 2015, Shin has elevated this annual event into a cultural phenomenon, attracting over 930,000 visitors to SoHo by 2023. Under her guidance, deTour has evolved from being more than just an event; it has become a movement.

In 2022, Shin expanded her visionary reach by launching ‘FabriX', Hong Kong's pioneering venture into digital fashion. In just a year, FabriX has already made a significant impact at London and Paris Fashion Week, featuring a cutting-edge AR kiosk where visitors can virtually try on designer pieces and pre-order limited-edition merchandise—all in a seamless, innovative experience.

Shin envisions the future of consumerism as an evolving landscape. For her, digital fashion transcends visual aesthetics; it serves as a powerful storytelling medium that advocates sustainability, inclusivity, and the freedom to express one's identity both physically and virtually.

Shin's curatorial portfolio is a testament to her avant-garde vision: from the haunting beauty of Ivana Wong's The Singing Canvases to the raw energy of Yamaguchi Soichi's “The Way You Look”, and the mind-bending Unparalleled Madness by Wang Qingsong. She's also the creative force behind “It is NOW” by Tom Binns, “PEDDERZINE Uncatalogued” by Theseus Chan for JOYCE, and PMQ's iconic “Smart Fashion Runway I & II”, among others.

Adonian Chan

Adonian Chan, Hong Kong-born type designer, graphic designer and design curator.


He has been studying Hong Kong Beiwei “Zansyu” font style and digitising it into computer fonts since 2011, and co-authored the book A Study on Hong Kong Beiwei Calligraphy & Type Design alongside design editor Ire Tsui in 2018. Documenting eight years of research on Hong Kong Beiwei and his type design “Zansyu”, the publication earned the Grand Award and the Distinguished Publishing Awards at the 2019 Hong Kong Publishing Biennial Awards. The type design also received accolades, including the Typeface Design Award from the New York Type Directors Club and the Excellent Work from the Tokyo Type Director’s Club.


Adonian was the recipient of the 2019 DFA Hong Kong Young Design Talent Award. He has been invited to speak at various notable events, including Apple Hong Kong, Typographics New York City (2021), Letterform Archive San Francisco and Dongdaemun Design Plaza in Seoul. His work has been widely covered by media outlets such as Idea Magazine (Japan) and CNN (US). He also co-curated the Hong Kong design festival deTour in both 2020 and 2021.

Key Visual Design

Whiteground

WHITEGROUND is a multi-disciplinary design practice striving for consistency with refinement. A team of seasoned and forward-thinking architects and designers, we work to break the conventions with our expertize in architecture, interior design, branding, and graphic design.

Seeking a consistent brand image across our work, we design through cross-media innovation and provide immersive design solutions. The processes of trial and experiment are important to us, for discovering new ways of storytelling and identity. We aim to challenge norms by exploring the use of visual expressions in spatial design contexts that exceed people’s expectations.  To us, “white”, like a canvas, represents a space where the creative process begins; while “ground” bears the meaning of essence. We aim for our works to be created on the basis of pure motivation.

Napp Studio & Architects

Napp Studio & Architects is a design office that works across scales and disciplines between space and object – from architecture, interior, exhibition to furniture and product. We, as Architects, approach design subjects through series and layers of rigorous operations, e.g. social, cultural, formal, practical, structural and environmental, that are translated into different intentions in all scales.

The practice has won multiple local and international awards including Gold Award in Taipei International Design Award, Golden Pin Award, Perspective’s 40 under 40, and Hong Kong Young Design Talent Award; and their works have been exhibited in various exhibitions world-widely, e.g. International Venice Architecture Biennale, SPARK The Science and Art of Creativity, Hong Kong/Shen Zhen Bi-City Biennale of Urbanism/Architecture.
