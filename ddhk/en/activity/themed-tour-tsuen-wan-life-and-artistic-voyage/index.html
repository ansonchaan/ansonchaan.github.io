Themed Tour: Tsuen Wan Life and Artistic Voyage

Dates
15.12.2024 (Sun)
19.01.2025 (Sun)
Time
14:00-16:00
Assembly Point
Sai Lau Kok Garden (outside the Multi-purpose Room)
Cultural Tour Specialist: Kit ChanTour Expert: Walk in Hong Kong
Led by Kit Chan, a columnist, editor, photographer, and independent curator, this tour will guide participants through the #ddhk creative installations at Sai Lau Kok Garden, Sam Tung Uk Museum, and Tsuen Wan Park, where participants will delve deep into the creative concepts of the works and discover the unique features of these landmarks. Kit will also take participants on a tour of Yi Pei Square, the “communal living space” and Nina Park, an extraordinary wood fossil park, exploring the diverse public spaces and experiencing the creative vibe of Tsuen Wan.
Note
  • - The guided tour is conducted in Cantonese supplemented with English and Putonghua.
  • - Shuttle bus service will be provided to transport participants between selected attractions.
  • - Registration for tours in January 2025 will begin at 10am on 12 December 2024 (Thu).
About Kit Chan
Graduated from the University of Hong Kong, and formerly a journalist, Kit Chan currently works as a column writer, editor, photographer and independent curator.
Putting focus on architecture, humanity, space making and urbanism, he actively documents Hong Kong, as well as other cities.

He is the author of two books, KNOWHERE and KNOWHERE HONG KONG, published respectively in 2020 and 2023.
The former publication has been awarded with Hong Kong Publishing Biennial Awards 2021.
About Walk in Hong Kong
Walk in Hong Kong is a renowned cultural enterprise offering meticulously crafted walking tours for those seeking to uncover the hidden facets of Hong Kong. Their immersive experiences blend entertainment, education, and a celebration of the city's captivating tales. With expertise in both physical and virtual realms, they curate engaging journeys offline and online. Their passionate guides, hailing from diverse professional backgrounds, share personal stories and promote an authentic Hong Kong beyond guidebooks.
